Black Pepper Tofu

Prep Time Cook Time Total Time
25 mins 30 mins 55 mins

Ingredients

  • 411 g extra-firm tofu
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • — black pepper sauce —
  • 118 ml soy sauce
  • 3 tsp black pepper
  • 79 ml agave or maple syrup
  • 59 ml rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • — vegetables —
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small red onion sliced
  • 2 rib celery, sliced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp fresh grated ginger

Instructions

  1. Wrap the tofu in paper towels (or a clean dry tea towel). Place a plate or pan on top of the wrapped tofu, and put a couple of heavy books or cast iron pan on top of that. Let the tofu press for about 30 minutes. You can skip this step if you buy the super firm variety in a vacuum pack.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Slice the tofu into about 4-6 slices, then rip each slice into bite sized chunks. You want a ripped texture; it makes the tofu taste crispy and amazing!
  4. Add the tofu pieces to a large bowl, then add the olive oil, cornstarch, salt and pepper and stir gently to combine. You want the tofu to be coated.
  5. Arrange in an even layer on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, until golden and crispy on the outside. Remove from the oven and set aside.
  6. — Cook the vegetables and finish —
  7. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, warm the olive oil
  8. Add the onion, bell pepper and celery and sauté for 3-5 minutes, until the vegetables brighten in color but not until they are super soft.
  9. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for one more minute, stirring constantly.
  10. Add the baked tofu to the skillet with the vegetables. Stir the sauce one last time before pouring into the skillet.
  11. Stir to combine, and cook for another minute. The sauce will thicken slightly.
  12. Serve with rice and enjoy!
